## Different definitions and views of “institutions”?
- Institutions are the beliefs and modes of conduct instituted by the collectivity”, 
(Emile Durkheim, 1895, in “The rules of sociological method”)
- Institutions are a regularity in social behavior that is agreed to by all members of a society, specifies behavior in specific recurrent situations, and is either self-policed or policed by some external authority” 
(Schotter, 1981, p.11)
- Institutions are the rules of the game in society” or “the humanly devised constraints that shape human interaction” 
(North, 1990, p.3)
- Institutions are a system of social factors – such as rules, beliefs, norms, and organizations – that guide, enable and constrain the actions of individuals 
(Greif, 2000)
- Institutions are the laws, informal rules, and conventions that give a durable structure to social interactions among members of a population
Bowles (2003, p 47-48)
